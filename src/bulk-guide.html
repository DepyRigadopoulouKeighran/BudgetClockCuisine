Cooking in Bulk : A Step By Step Guide

Bulk cooking can take the form of preparing substantial quantities of various ingredients that can be mixed and matched, or crafting generous portions of specific mixed dishes, such as lasagna or casseroles. We'll delve deeper into these variations later on. Regardless of your chosen approach, there are key steps to embark upon:

STEP 1 - PLAN YOUR MEALS

Begin by determining the number of meals you aim to create for the week and the corresponding recipes. Your choice hinges on whether you opt for the mix-and-match style of batch cooking or prefer to focus on a couple of larger mixed dishes. For inspiration, our Foodal platform offers an array of recipes tailor-made for batch cooking. Additionally, Pinterest and Google searches can yield fresh bulk cooking ideas, often featuring sale items, seasonal ingredients, and pantry staples. Grains bowls, sheet pan meals, and pasta dishes align well with the mix-and-match approach. Meanwhile, larger batches of mixed dishes like lasagna, enchilada casseroles, chili, and soup work wonders.

STEP 2 - MAKE YOUR LIST AND SHOP

With your culinary plans in place, it's time to put pen to paper. Group your grocery items according to their location within the store to streamline your shopping experience. Sticking to your list ensures you buy what you need without excess waste. Keep an eye out for discounted staples like olive oil, spices, canned goods, and grains. Remember to shop your own pantry first – you can utilize rice, beans, lentils, and frozen ingredients that are already stocked. Additionally, make use of stored frozen fruits, vegetables, and grains.

STEP 3 – PREP AND COOK

Equipped with your ingredients, it's time to dive in! Choose a day when you can dedicate 1 to 2 hours for cooking and cleanup. Sundays often provide ideal windows, offering moments to cook while enjoying a movie or sports game.

BATCH COOKING METHODS

The manner of your preparation hinges on your chosen bulk cooking style. Let's delve into the specifics of each method:
For Mix-and-Match Style: This approach involves preparing individual components that can be combined into diverse meals throughout the week. It's ideal for dishes like grain bowls, sheet pan meals, and pasta dishes.
For Larger Mixed Dishes: For those who prefer crafting larger portions of specific mixed dishes, this method works wonders for dishes such as lasagna, enchilada casseroles, chili, and soup. By adhering to these steps and methods, you're poised to embrace the art of bulk cooking with ease and efficiency.
