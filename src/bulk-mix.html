MIX-AND-MATCH MEAL STRATEGY

When aiming to savor a diverse array of flavors during the week without expanding your grocery list excessively, crafting mix-and-match meals presents an ideal solution. This approach centers on preparing one or two grains, proteins, a variety of vegetables, and multiple sauces. These components can then be artfully combined to create distinct meals. While certain core ingredients remain constant, the key lies in having an assortment of sauces, cheeses, or spice blends that provide distinctive flavor profiles to each meal. My preferred combination blueprint is as follows:

- 2 Protein Choices: beef, chicken, tofu, chickpeas, fish, etc.
- 2 Carbohydrate Options: brown rice, quinoa, farro, whole wheat pasta, whole wheat tortillas, sweet potatoes, etc.
- A Selection of Sauces: peanut sauce, barbecue, teriyaki, marinara, green curry, salsa, etc.
- An Assortment of Vegetables: including leafy greens and roasted root vegetables, among others.

In instances where time is tight, opting for a bag of pre-mixed fresh vegetables works splendidly.
Alternatively, choose in-season or on-sale produce for added variety. Once you've pinpointed your selections in each category, it's time to cook and prepare ample quantities.
The quantity depends on the number of meals you intend to prepare in advance for the week and the servings required per meal. From there, you have two choices: store the components separately for mixing and matching throughout the week, or pre-portion them in containers on Sunday for effortless access.

MIX-AND-MATCH PREP SAMPLE MENU COOKING INSTRUCTIONS:

STEP 1 - START WITH THE GRAINS
Cook farro on the stovetop, or use a rice cooker or Instant Pot. Roast sweet potatoes in the oven or steam/microwave as an alternative.

STEP 2 - COOK VEGETABLES AND PROTEINS
Toss chopped vegetables in oil, salt, and pepper, then roast in the oven. Stir-fry tofu and chicken on the stove or roast them alongside the vegetables (using separate baking sheets if necessary).

STEP 3 - PREPARE UNCOOKED COMPONENTS AND INITIATE CLEANUP
While cooking, drain and rinse canned beans if necessary. Prepare sauces (like peanut sauce) and begin tidying up.

STEP 4 - COOL, ADD EXTRAS, AND STORE
Once cooked, you can store each component separately or pre-portion the meals. Items such as cheese, avocado slices, yogurt, or sour cream, as well as spice blends, can be added during portioning or just before eating. Ensure all ingredients cool to room temperature before storing in the fridge or freezer.

MIXED-DISH MEALS
Bulk cooking is also applicable to mixed-dish meals like enchiladas, chili, and casseroles. Prepare a substantial batch of one or two recipes, freeze them in individual portions, and enjoy the convenience of pre-cooked meals ready for thawing and reheating. This approach is advantageous for utilizing sale items or ingredients nearing expiration. For instance, making two trays of lasagna when ricotta is on sale offers a week's meal along with slices stored in the freezer for future consumption.

TO EXCEL IN LARGE-BATCH MIXED-DISH COOKING:

1. Choose recipes with overlapping ingredients to save time and expenses.
2. Slightly undercook vegetables and pasta to prevent excessive softening upon reheating.
3. Use caution when doubling spices; start with moderate quantities and adjust to taste.
4. Allow dishes to cool fully before storing.
5. Divide into smaller portions for efficient reheating and reduced food waste.
6. Use airtight containers or freezer bags to prevent freezer burn.
7. Label containers with recipe names, preparation dates, and reheating instructions. Certain foods (fried items, high-water-content fruits/vegetables, or mayonnaise/yogurt-based sauces) are less freezer-friendly.
8. Batch cooking for mixed-dish meals also suits weeks with lighter schedules, enabling preparation of freezer-friendly meals for busier weeks.
9. Think to invest in a big freezer
